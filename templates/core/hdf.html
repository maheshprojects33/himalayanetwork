Project Overview

Understanding the Initiative

🏠

Background

Nepal, the Himalayan country in South Asia lies between India and China. It is located in the Himalayas and contains eight of the world's ten highest peaks. Although the country is relatively small and it just covers 147,181 square kilometers, eighty percent of its territory is occupied by the dramatic peaks of the Himalayas and Mountains. It is the home to Mount Everest and the Birthplace of the Lord Buddha. The government system is a federal democratic republic; the chief of the state is the president, and the head of the government is the prime minister. Its population is nearly 30 million and per capita income of Nepal is the 728 dollars.

💥

Effects by Earthquake

Geophysicists and other experts had warned for decades that Nepal was vulnerable to a deadly earthquake, particularly because of its geology, urbanization, and architecture. But no one ever thought that 25 April 2015 will be that much black or painful day for Nepal and Nepalese; the people of Nepal have faced really shivering moment in those days and unfortunately, the results are everlasting in terms of different points of view. This major earthquake killed nearly 8,800 people and injured nearly 22,000. Its epicenter was east of Gorkha District at Barpak, Gorkha, and its hypocenter was at a depth of approximately 8.2 km. The earthquake triggered an avalanche on Mount Everest. Hundreds of thousands of Nepalese were made homeless with entire villages flattened, across many districts of the country, world heritage historical monuments, Centuries old buildings, temples were destroyed. The pain of losing family members and the problem of homes and food had worsened the situation of Nepal and Nepalese.

📌

Project Identification

After sudden devastating earthquake in Nepal, we started to survey the most affected and most measurable places where immediate helping hand should apply. We have consulted and took advises from different people to find for target place. Another major cause to identifying any new project place is the weak financial condition of that place who never can rebuild their home in life time.


According to HDF identifying criteria, we have selected another most affected place by earthquake and poverty that our team had found is at Gokarneshwor Village, Ward no 2. The local community had strongly recommended building homes on their own land for the earthquake victims. The plan started for 10 homes where 37 numbers of people will be assisted in this Terrace Land at hilly station at Gokarneshwor.

⚠️

Statement of the Problem

Unfortunately, all of sudden, the devastating earthquake of 25 April 2015 occurred in Nepal have become a historical misery for Nepalese people leading them to severe plight. Fifteen districts were badly affected; the pain of having to lose their family members and the problem of homes and food had worsened the situation. So, the temporary shelter was the primary need for the victims with the starting of monsoon. Besides this earthquake, another main problem of this place is very poor financial condition due different family and social causes like loss of main person of family, no proper job etc.


We have started the analysis for target people according to our objectives and we have planned to immediately implementation of the action in work. We have faced several problems while we were in targeted place Gokarneshwor Village. Conditions we have found are much more risky and heart rending which was beyond our thoughts

Objectives of the Project

  • ✓ To support the victims and get back them into normal life.
  • ✓ To creating base for their life where they can stand by themselves through their own capabilities.
  • ✓ Create earthquake-resistant structures following modern building standards
  • ✓ Provide long-term sustainable housing solutions
  • ✓ Last but very essential objectives was in this place is to build permanent homes at victims' own land, who have no chance to build their own home in their lifetime due to poverty.

Conclusion

The Himalaya Dāna Foundation, beneficiaries and all the Nepalese are very much grateful to His Serene Highness Prince Albert II, the Honorary Consul Mr. Moghadam and all the people in Monaco for their generous support for the Gokarneshwor Earthquake Relief Home Project. It is a matter of relief that the project was successfully completed as per plan within the planned period of time and it has provided the earthquake victim families with the lovely homes and helped them to live better lives. It has been a precious lifelong gift for the people in grief from the warm hearted people of the Principality of Monaco. Furthermore, the co-operation of local people, leaders, village development committee personnel and municipality were also appreciable for their cooperation. As a matter of fact, the success in initial stage has been encouraging us to go further towards achieving our mission of helping earthquake victims, bringing peace and happiness to them. Therefore, HDF plans to replicate the project and expand the project area.

Finally, Happiness exists on earth, and it is won through prudent exercise of generosity. And the practice of generosity had been done by people from Monaco for the sake of Nepalese people who have got way to overcome the grief from earthquake. The warm hearted people from Monaco for their direct and indirect generous contribution are spreading the bright light with peace and harmony to the life of the needy people in their dark time. Undeniably, this accomplished work is a kind of great work, standing here as a perfect example at the Gokarneshwor. This work is the ideal model work which obviously enhances our excellent bilateral relation between Monaco and Nepal. This merit of right help to right people will transfer to all of you directly and indirectly.
